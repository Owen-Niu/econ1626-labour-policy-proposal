# 4. Recommended Package and Implementation Plan

I recommend combining Options 1 and 3 into an 'Australian AI Workforce Compact'. Reskilling without employer accountability results in credentials but no employment, and employer obligations without a training infrastructure are merely empty mandates. SWSP builds the pipeline, and skills guarantee generates demand by keeping people longer than displacing them. Option 2's accounts are suitable for the SWSP partnership, but they should not take the lead.

## Phase 1 (Months 1-12)

Establish SWSP AI Transition Partnerships ($200m per year, 1:1 matching). Embed the Skills Guarantee in the Fair Work Act with a 12-month grace period. Build an AI Labour Market Observatory at Jobs and Skills Australia.

## Phase 2 (Months 13-24)

Pilot portable training accounts ($3,000 per worker, accredited courses only) at SWSP partner companies. Scale Skills Guarantee Enforcement via Random Audits. Target Outreach to Workers Aged 45+ and Regional Areas.

## Phase 3 (Months 25-36)

Measure training completion, employment at 6 and 12 months, and changes in wages. Adjust Sectoral Priorities Based on Data.

## Key Performance Indicators

| **Indicator** | **Target** |
| --- | --- |
| Workers completing AI-transition training annually | 45,000 by Year 2 |
| Large firms with documented redeployment protocols | 80% by Year 2 |
| Employment rate of training completers at 6 months | 75% |

Gross Cost: $340 million per year. Offsetting savings -- reduced income support ($85m) and increased tax revenue -- net cost of $255 million.
